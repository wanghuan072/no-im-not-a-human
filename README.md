## üéÆ About the Game

**No I'm Not a Human** is a psychological horror visual novel where extreme solar activity has made daytime survival impossible. As civilization collapses, you play as a survivor barricaded in an apartment, trying to survive the night by identifying dangerous **Visitors** disguised as humans seeking shelter.

The game explores themes of trust, paranoia, and survival in extreme circumstances, where the greatest threat might be the person you choose to help. Every decision is critical, as letting in the wrong person could be fatal.

### Game Story

As the sun draws closer to Earth, civilization has collapsed. The extreme heat during the day makes outdoor survival impossible, forcing the remaining population to seek shelter. But something else emerged in this new world - visitors who can mimic human appearance and behavior, but whose intentions remain unknown and deadly.

You must survive by identifying who is human and who is a visitor. Your survival depends on making the right choices, managing limited resources, and maintaining your sanity in this paranoid nightmare.

### Complex Multi-Branching Ending System
The game features over 10 different ending paths, where each player's choice creates a butterfly effect, influencing the story's final direction. This design makes every gameplay experience unique, greatly enhancing the game's replay value and exploration fun.

### Three-Night Progressive Horror Challenge
The game cleverly divides the horror experience into three nights, with each night's difficulty and complexity increasing progressively. Players need to learn to identify real and fake visitors, master survival skills, and make correct judgments under pressure.

### Deep Psychological Horror Experience
No I'm Not a Human doesn't rely on cheap jump scares, but creates sustained tension through carefully constructed atmosphere, subtle sound design, and clever visual cues, allowing players to experience true psychological horror.

### Energy Management System
You have limited daily energy for investigating guests. Each inspection of body parts, conversation, or action costs energy. You must balance investigation needs with energy conservation to survive.

### Visitor Identification Mechanics
Master the art of identifying dangerous visitors through physical traits, behavioral patterns, and investigation techniques. Every detail matters when your life is on the line.

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### What is No I'm Not a Human?
No I'm Not a Human is a psychological horror visual novel where extreme solar activity has made daytime survival impossible. You must survive the night by identifying dangerous Visitors disguised as humans seeking shelter in your apartment, making critical decisions that determine your fate.

### Who developed the game?
The game was developed by Russian indie studio Trioskaz and published by Critical Reflex. It's available on Steam for Windows PC, with console versions planned for future release.

### How does the energy system work?
You have limited daily energy for investigating guests. Each inspection of body parts, conversation, or action costs energy. You can eat, drink, or rest to pass time, but must balance investigation needs with energy conservation.

### What makes this game unique?
The game combines psychological horror with moral dilemmas and resource management. You must make split-second decisions about who to trust, with each choice having potentially deadly consequences in this atmospheric post-apocalyptic setting.

### How many endings are there?
The game features multiple endings based on your choices and interactions with visitors. Each playthrough can lead to different outcomes depending on how you handle the mysterious visitors and manage your survival strategy.

### Where can I play it?
No I'm Not a Human is available on Steam for Windows PC. Console versions are planned for future release. A demo is available on Steam for players to try before purchasing.

### Are there Roblox experiences available?
Yes! The No I'm Not a Human Roblox community has created various fan-made experiences inspired by the original game. These games capture the core mechanics of visitor identification, survival horror, and moral decision-making in a multiplayer environment.
